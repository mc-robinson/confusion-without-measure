**Microsomal Stability Assay reporting details checklist:**

	🙾 Test compound concentration used (often 1 µM) noted in procedure

    🙾 Protein concentration used (often in mg/mL) noted in procedure

    🙾 Volume of incubation used (often in µL) noted in procedure

	🙾 Avoid only reporting t1/2 or % remaining at a single time point, if possible (these quantities depend greatly on assay conditions, and are hard to compare across assay setups).

	🙾 Always report the raw observed CLint,app in µL ᐧ min−1 ᐧ mg−1 along with any scaled values

	🙾 Report fu,mics if measured and used in computing reported scaled values

	🙾 The methodology for any scaled CL or CLint values should be clearly detailed along with assumed values of constants used such as microsomal protein density (usually in mg/g), liver mass (usually in g/kg), and Qh in the species of interest.

**Hepatocyte Stability Assay reporting details checklist:**

	🙾 Test compound concentration used (often 1 µM) noted in procedure

    🙾 Cell density used (often in million cells/mL) noted in procedure

    🙾 Volume of incubation used (often in µL) noted in procedure

	🙾 Avoid only reporting t1/2 or % remaining at a single time point, if possible (these quantities depend greatly on assay conditions, and are hard to understand across assay setups).

	🙾 Always report the raw observed CLint,app in µL ᐧ min−1 ᐧ million cells−1 along with any scaled values

	🙾 Report fu,heps if measured and used in computing reported scaled values

	🙾 The methodology for any scaled CL or CLint values should be clearly detailed along with assumed values of constants used such as hepatocellularity (usually in million cells/g), liver mass (usually in g/kg), and Qh in the species of interest.
