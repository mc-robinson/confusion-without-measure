**Preclinical Efficacy Data reporting details checklist:**

	🙾 For compounds going into efficacy models, the PK should be reported for at least the same doses used in the efficacy study, using the same route of administration.

	🙾 Plasma protein binding in the efficacy model species should be measured to help model free concentration profiles during the efficacy experiments. 

	🙾 Binding in the assay media used for in vitro PD experiments should be measured if possible to understand the true in vitro PD response corrected for non-specific binding.

	🙾 Dose levels used in the efficacy study should be justified using prior in vivo PK and in vitro PD experiments as best as possible.

	🙾 PK/PD modeling should assume the free-drug hypothesis holds unless specific evidence shows otherwise. I.e, unbound concentrations should be used to model in vivo exposure.

  🙾 If there is suspicion that the free-drug hypothesis may not hold (due to active transport into/out of the organs of interest or other reasons), then concentrations measured in the tissue of interest should be reported *with* non-specific binding in that tissue to understand unbound exposure at the site of action.
